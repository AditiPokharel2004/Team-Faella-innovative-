Basic Recipes

Omelette

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 small tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon mustard oil or any cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, crack the eggs and add the chopped onion, green chili, tomato, and coriander leaves.
  2. Add turmeric powder and salt to the mixture. Beat everything together until well combined.
  3. Heat the mustard oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the pan, spreading it evenly.
  5. Cook for 2-3 minutes on one side until the bottom is golden brown.
  6. Flip the omelet and cook the other side for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Once fully cooked, transfer the omelet to a plate and serve hot with a side of rice or as a snack.

Potato Fry(Aalu Fry)

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped/li>
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green chili, chopped (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds,coriander seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. the minced garlic and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the chopped onion and green chili, and sauté until the onion turns soft and translucent.
  4. Add the diced potatoes to the pan. Stir well to coat them with the oil and spices.
  5. Sprinkle turmeric powder, coriander powder, chili powder, and salt over the potatoes. Mix everything well.
  6. Cover the pan and cook on low heat for about 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are tender and slightly crispy.
  7. Remove the lid and increase the heat to medium-high. Fry the potatoes for an additional 2-3 minutes to get a nice crispy texture.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably leftover)
  • 1 small carrot, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add chopped onions. Sauté until soft.
  2. Add the chopped carrot and peas, and cook for a few minutes until tender.
  3. If using eggs, push the veggies to one side of the pan and scramble the eggs on the other side.
  4. Add the cooked rice, turmeric powder, soy sauce, and salt. Mix everything well.
  5. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve warm.

Dal Bhat (Lentil Soup with Rice)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup split lentils (masoor dal or moong dal)
  • 2 cup of water
  • 1 small onion ,chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1-2 dried red chilies (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the lentils and soak them in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and dried red chilies. Let them splutter.
  3. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until the onion turns golden.
  4. Add the turmeric powder.
  5. Add the soaked lentils and salt to the pot, and pour in enough water to cover the lentils.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the lentils are cooked and the dal reaches your desired consistency.
  7. Serve the dal hot with steamed rice (bhat) and a side of vegetables or pickles.

Pickle

Ingredients(Tomato Pickle):

  • 4 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon mustard oil
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Heat mustard oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the fenugreek seeds and let them turn dark.
  2. Add the minced garlic and ginger to the pan, and sauté until golden brown
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes, green chilies, turmeric powder, cumin powder, and salt. Stir well and cook until the tomatoes soften and the mixture thickens.
  4. Add chili powder if you prefer a spicier pickle. Cook for a few more minutes until the oil separates from the tomato mixture.
  5. Remove the pan from heat, and let it cool slightly.
  6. Add lemon juice and mix well. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  7. Serve the tomato pickle fresh, or store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Cauliflower Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil or any cooking oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook until it becomes soft and translucent.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and release their juices.
  4. Stir in the turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and chili powder. Cook for a couple of minutes.
  5. Add the cauliflower florets to the pan. Stir to coat them with the spices and tomato mixture.
  6. Add a little water (about 1/4 cup) to help the cauliflower cook. Cover the pan and cook on low heat for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cauliflower is tender.
  7. Once the cauliflower is cooked, sprinkle garam masala (if using) and mix well. Adjust salt to taste.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.
